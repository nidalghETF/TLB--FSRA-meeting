TOKEN LEBNANI - PRE-APPLICATION LEGAL FRAMEWORK

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Token Lebnani Ltd ("TLB") proposes a three-tier legal structure to facilitate regulated investment in Lebanese real estate through an ADGM-licensed Private Financing Platform. This memo outlines the legal framework for cross-border enforcement, asset protection, and regulatory compliance between ADGM and Lebanese jurisdictions.

Capital Framework (FSRA Final Ruling):

  • Base Capital: USD 10,000 (Category 4 minimum under PRU 3.4.1(1)(a), conditional on evidence)
  • Enforcement Litigation Reserve: USD 300,000 Day 1, phased to USD 575,000 (36 months)
  • Wind-Down Reserve: USD 100,000 (Standby Servicer-managed)
  • Total Day 1 Financial Requirement: USD 410,000

Primary Legal Mechanisms:

  • ADGM SPV ownership of Lebanese SARL for asset segregation
  • Treaty-based enforcement via the 1958 New York Convention
  • Registered security interests under Lebanese law

For your consideration: Non-Consolidation Opinion Requirement

The Project SPV structure relies on bankruptcy remoteness, which requires a formal Non-Consolidation Legal Opinion at the operational stage. This opinion is critical for investors and regulators. The legal team should be tasked with identifying appropriate counsel to provide this opinion and confirming whether it can be obtained pre-launch or must be obtained before first SPV formation.

CAPITAL EVIDENCE REQUIREMENTS (FSRA CONDITIONS)

The USD 10,000 Base Capital classification is conditional on providing the following evidence with the formal application:

1. System Architecture Audit Report: Third-party audit confirming that the payment initiation interface is technically incapable of receiving or instructing the receipt of client funds until the smart contract validates both the expiration of the 48-hour cooling-off period and the client's final "proceed" instruction.

2. VASP Partner Agreement: Agreement explicitly confirming that the VASP Partner maintains segregated client money accounts; the VASP acts as sole recipient and custodian of client funds from payment instruction; TLB has no ability to divert or access client funds; TLB has no authority to issue payment instructions except through pre-encoded smart contracts.

Regulatory Consequence: Any operational deviation from the non-custodial payment flow will be treated as holding Client Money, retroactively triggering the USD 50,000 Base Capital requirement under PRU 3.4.1(1)(b) and constituting a material license breach.

STANDBY SERVICER REQUIREMENT (FSRA MANDATORY)

The FSRA categorically rejected self-administered wind-down. The executed Standby Servicer Agreement is a mandatory component of the formal application.

Key Requirements:

  • ADGM-licensed Corporate Service Provider contracted as Standby Servicer
  • Responsibilities: SPV administration, asset liquidation coordination, cross-border coordination, dispute resolution, regulatory reporting
  • Activation: Automatic upon wind-down trigger event
  • Fee Structure: Activation fee, monthly retainer, contingency budget managed within USD 100,000 Wind-Down Reserve

The executed Standby Servicer Agreement will be submitted with the formal application.
