REGULATORY BUSINESS PLAN (RBP)

Document 1: Executive Summary — Token Lebnani

Introduction and Business Concept

Token Lebnani Ltd ("TLB" or "the Platform") proposes to establish a regulated digital securities platform enabling diaspora investors to acquire ownership interests in Lebanese real estate assets through a compliant ADGM framework. The platform addresses a significant market gap: Lebanese diaspora investors seeking to maintain connection to their home country through property ownership currently lack access to regulated, secure investment vehicles that provide the transparency, governance, and enforceability they require.

The platform's core value proposition is "hands-free" property ownership—enabling investors to acquire real estate assets in Lebanon without the management burdens, cross-border enforcement challenges, and banking obstacles that have traditionally prevented diaspora participation. By structuring investments through bankruptcy-remote Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs) incorporated in the Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM), Token Lebnani provides investors with the legal protections of a premier common law jurisdiction while gaining exposure to Lebanese property markets.

In addition to Regulatory Base Capital, Token Lebnani maintains a Wind-Down Reserve of USD 100,000, which forms part of the overall wind-down budget managed by the contracted Standby Servicer. This reserve covers professional fees, regulatory obligations, and essential services required during the wind-down period. This reserve ensures that, in the event of business cessation, Token Lebnani has sufficient liquid resources to effect an orderly wind-down, complete remaining investor obligations, and discharge regulatory responsibilities. The Wind-Down Reserve is maintained in cash or highly liquid instruments accessible within standard banking timeframes, is segregated from operational accounts, and is not utilized for day-to-day business activities.

Regulatory Positioning and License Scope

Token Lebnani seeks a Category 4 Financial Services Permission under the Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA) framework. The Category 4 license authorizes the regulated activity of "Arranging Deals in Investments," which enables TLB to facilitate primary market issuance of digital securities representing ownership in real estate assets located in Lebanon.

The Category 4 license scope is explicitly limited to arranging activities. TLB does not operate and is not authorized to operate a Multilateral Trading Facility (MTF), Exchange, or Custodian. This distinction is fundamental to the platform's regulatory positioning and capital structure. The Category 4 framework provides significant capital efficiency advantages compared to higher-tier licenses while enabling the platform to offer institutional-grade investment infrastructure for digital securities.

Under the Private Financing Platform (PFP) framework applicable within the ADGM RegLab environment, TLB serves Retail Clients subject to the PFP retail exemption, which permits a maximum of 200 Retail Clients per asset offering without requiring a full prospectus. This exemption enables TLB to serve the Lebanese diaspora market through appropriate knowledge-based qualification requirements and enhanced suitability and disclosure standards.

The "Bankrupt-Proof SPV" Structural Model

Token Lebnani operates a "Bankrupt-Proof SPV" model, meaning that each Special Purpose Vehicle incorporated in the ADGM serves as a passive holding vehicle without active operational management responsibilities, banking relationships, or operational infrastructure. The SPV's sole function is to hold legal title to the underlying property and issue digital tokens representing ownership interests to investors.

This structural model provides several operational and regulatory advantages. First, the passive nature of the SPV eliminates the need for the SPV to maintain banking relationships, employ staff, or conduct operational activities in Lebanon. Second, the SPV's bankruptcy-remote structure protects investors from operational liabilities arising from property management activities. Third, the clear separation between ownership (SPV) and operations (Facilities Management Company) enables regulatory compliance in a jurisdiction where TLB does not maintain physical presence.

The SPV structure is implemented through a three-tier hierarchy: the ADGM-incorporated SPV holds 100% of the shares of a Lebanese SARL (Société à Responsabilité Limitée), which in turn holds legal title to the physical property. This structure enables enforcement through the New York Convention (to which Lebanon is a signatory) while maintaining ADGM governance over the ownership structure.

Target Market and Investor Classification

Token Lebnani serves the Lebanese diaspora through a single-track qualification system for Retail Clients. The platform's target market is the Lebanese diaspora community—individuals with Lebanese nationality, heritage, or strong connection to Lebanon who reside outside the country and seek to maintain economic connection to their homeland through property ownership.

The qualification system is knowledge-based rather than wealth-exclusive. Rather than requiring all investors to demonstrate High-Net-Worth status (which would exclude the majority of the target diaspora market), TLB permits retail participation subject to knowledge-based qualification requirements. This approach aligns with the PFP regulatory framework while expanding access to investors who can demonstrate requisite understanding of the investment product.

Retail Clients access the platform through the PFP retail exemption, subject to a maximum of 200 Retail Clients per asset offering and mandatory knowledge-based qualification through the Mastery Assessment. Retail Clients may invest at tiered minimum amounts in the mid-to-low five-figure USD range (specifically, USD 10,000 to USD 55,000 per asset).

The Mastery Assessment is a comprehensive evaluation designed to verify that Retail Clients possess requisite understanding of the investment product, covering risk recognition, asset structure comprehension, and platform mechanics. Additionally, the Self-Declaration Protocol requires clients to acknowledge the risks of the investment in plain language, with interactive comprehension checks ensuring informed consent.

Target investors are defined based on investment intent and motivation rather than demographic factors. Legacy Investors are diaspora investors seeking to maintain connection to Lebanon through property ownership, prioritizing stability and capital preservation. These investors typically possess deep familiarity with Lebanese market conditions, cultural context, and historical economic patterns, which informs their understanding of investment risks and opportunities. Passive Beneficiaries are investors seeking hands-free property exposure without management responsibilities, valuing the platform's operational infrastructure for revenue collection and distribution services.

Facilities Management Infrastructure

The Facilities Management Company (FMC) serves as the operational arm of each property SPV, responsible for all day-to-day property management functions that would otherwise require direct investor involvement or SPV administrative burden. The FMC enables the SPV structure to function as a passive holding vehicle while ensuring properties are actively maintained, tenants are managed, and revenues are collected and remitted in accordance with platform protocols.

The FMC operates under binding contractual obligations establishing performance standards and accountability mechanisms. Key obligations include fiduciary duty to the SPV for all collected revenues, maintenance of complete transaction records for a minimum of seven years, transparency and disclosure of any conflicts of interest or material changes in ownership or financial condition, cooperation with platform governance processes including investor voting and regulatory reporting, and maintenance of appropriate insurance coverage including professional indemnity and public liability insurance. FMC performance monitoring operates through a Token Holder Voting System that positions TLB as the contractual action arm of token holders rather than a discretionary manager.

**Token Holder Governance Framework:**

TLB's role is strictly limited to platform administration and contractual enforcement. TLB does not exercise discretionary judgment over FMC performance matters. Instead, TLB uploads quarterly KPI results and monthly performance reports to the platform, where token holders review the data and vote on whether they are satisfied with FMC performance.

**Governance Mechanics:**

Token holders receive quarterly KPI assessments uploaded to the platform by TLB, including property condition reports, revenue collection metrics, maintenance completion rates, and tenant satisfaction data where available. TLB provides a 14-day voting window during which token holders may vote to approve the FMC's performance, vote to apply automated penalty clauses for underperformance, or vote to initiate FMC termination proceedings.

**TLB's Role: Action Arm, Not Manager:**

TLB's function is to execute the collective decisions of token holders, not to independently manage or remediate FMC performance issues. The distinction is critical: TLB does not "remediate" underperformance through direct intervention. TLB applies automated penalty calculations as defined in the FMC Agreement when KPIs fall below thresholds. TLB initiates termination procedures when token holders vote to replace the FMC. TLB coordinates transition to backup FMC relationships when termination is approved. This structure ensures that TLB operates within Category 4 boundaries—facilitating investor decisions rather than making them.

**Automated Consequence Framework:**

The FMC Agreement incorporates automated penalty provisions that trigger when KPI thresholds are not met. These provisions are encoded in the contract and applied programmatically when conditions are satisfied, eliminating discretionary judgment by TLB. Penalties are calculated as fixed percentages of FMC fees as defined in the agreement, not as negotiated remedies. Token holders vote to approve or reject penalty applications through the platform governance mechanism.

The FMC is selected, contracted, and monitored by TLB on behalf of the SPV following a qualitative assessment based on demonstrated capabilities. Selection criteria include operational capability (personnel, communication systems, and operational infrastructure), local market knowledge (rental market awareness, vendor availability, and regulatory requirements), financial management competence (rent collection tracking, expense documentation, and remittance reporting), technology and reporting capability (communication tools and monthly reporting requirements), and regulatory awareness (local property management requirements and compliance obligations). The FMC's primary obligation is to the SPV, with performance standards and reporting requirements established in the FMC Agreement and monitored through the platform's governance infrastructure. Key responsibilities include revenue collection and management, tenant management, property maintenance and repairs, vendor coordination, regulatory compliance and tax administration, and quarterly reporting.

The FMC Agreement includes defined termination provisions protecting all parties and ensuring continuity of property management. For-cause termination permits immediate termination upon material breach, fraud, gross negligence, or sustained performance failure, with transition protocol requirements including knowledge transfer and documentation handover. For-convenience termination permits either party to terminate upon ninety days written notice, subject to transition obligations and fee payment through the termination date. The platform maintains backup FMC relationships to ensure property management continuity in case of FMC termination.

Token Lebnani operates under the principle that outsourcing operational functions to third parties does not outsource regulatory responsibility. Under ADGM GEN Rule 3.3 (Outsourcing), the regulated entity remains ultimately accountable to the Financial Services Regulatory Authority for all outsourced functions, regardless of contractual arrangements with service providers. Token Lebnani's liability for FMC performance is direct and non-delegable.

Token Lebnani's Category 4 license authorizes "Arranging Deals in Investments" and "Administering" activities. The platform maintains clear boundaries between permitted arranging activities and managing activities that would require higher-tier licensing. Permitted activities include strategic oversight and KPI setting, periodic review of property performance reports, adherence monitoring to ensure the FMC operates within agreed frameworks, and investor relations instruction regarding distribution schedules and communication protocols. Activities requiring Category 3C authorization, such as tactical day-to-day operational instructions or direct interaction with tenants and vendors, are avoided through structured governance protocols.

Token Lebnani structures its FMC arrangements to avoid classification as a Collective Investment Scheme by interposing itself as the sole counterparty to the FMC Agreement between investors and operational functions. Investors hold tokens representing ownership in the SPV and do not have direct operational control over the FMC. Investor rights are limited to corporate governance matters such as voting on major SPV transactions and receiving distributions, rather than operational property decisions.

Payment Infrastructure and Non-Custodial Model

Token Lebnani operates a strictly non-custodial model with respect to investor funds and property owner proceeds. All fund flows are processed through a UAE-licensed Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) partner, eliminating any touchpoint between the platform's operations and the Lebanese banking infrastructure. This architecture aligns with the platform's regulatory obligation to maintain separation of client assets.

The non-custodial model ensures that TLB never takes possession, control, or custody of investor funds at any stage of the transaction lifecycle. Investor funds flow from the investor to the Partner's corporate account, through the settlement ledger, to token distribution and owner distribution. TLB's sole banking relationship is a single corporate account for operational purposes.

Primary Market Investment Flow: Investors fund primary market purchases by sending fiat currency or accepted virtual assets to the Partner's UAE corporate account. The Partner credits the equivalent value to the settlement ledger and confirms investor verification status. Upon primary market close, the Partner executes token distribution to investor wallets and credits the property owner's distribution share to the settlement ledger.

FMC Revenue Collection and Remittance: The FMC maintains Lebanese banking relationships solely for the purpose of collecting rental income from local tenants. The FMC converts collected rents to USD and initiates international wire transfers to the Partner's UAE corporate account. The Partner reconciles and executes wallet distributions in accordance with the settlement cycle defined in the Partner Service Level Agreement.

Upon entity formation, Token Lebnani will execute a VASP Partner Agreement with a UAE-licensed Virtual Asset Service Provider. The agreement will confirm that the VASP Partner maintains segregated client money accounts, with no co-mingling of client funds and VASP operational funds. The agreement will explicitly confirm that Token Lebnani never takes possession, control, or custody of investor funds at any stage. Token Lebnani will have no authority to issue payment instructions directing the movement of client funds except through pre-encoded smart contracts for automated settlement. The VASP Partner will be responsible for KYC/AML verification, sanctions screening, and compliance with ADGM COBS Chapter 11 client asset requirements. A detailed framework document (TLB-VASP-FW-2026-001) is available upon request.

System Architecture Verification: Token Lebnani will submit a third-party system architecture audit report with the formal application, confirming that the payment initiation interface is technically incapable of receiving or instructing the receipt of client funds until the smart contract validates both the expiration of the 48-hour cooling-off period and the client's final "proceed" instruction.

Token Structure and Digital Securities

All tokens issued through the platform are Digital Securities under ADGM law. The token itself carries uniform characteristics: it represents direct ownership interest in the SPV, carries equivalent rights regardless of investment amount or qualification pathway, and is subject to the same regulatory treatment under ADGM law.

Tokens are issued using ERC-1400 or equivalent standards suitable for regulated securities. The standard enables transfer restrictions, investor whitelisting, and compliance controls embedded in the token contract. Before receiving tokens, investors must complete verification and be added to the platform's investor whitelist. The whitelist is maintained by the platform and updated based on verification status. Only whitelisted investors may receive tokens from primary or secondary market transactions.

Compliance and AML/CFT Framework

Token Lebnani maintains compliance infrastructure consistent with ADGM requirements, including AML/CFT policies, KYC procedures, transaction monitoring, and regulatory reporting capabilities. Compliance functions are managed by qualified personnel with appropriate expertise.

The AML/CFT Program is predicated on a risk-based approach that allocates resources in proportion to the assessed risk profile of customers, transactions, and geographic exposure. This approach recognizes that the Lebanese jurisdiction presents elevated AML/CFT risks arising from banking sector instability, FATF grey-list status, and the complexity of cross-border fund flows, and accordingly applies enhanced controls proportionate to these risk factors.

The AML/CFT governance structure comprises three lines of defense. The first line comprises business functions engaged in customer onboarding and transaction processing. The second line comprises the compliance function, including the MLRO, responsible for designing policies and monitoring effectiveness. The third line comprises internal audit, providing independent assurance on the framework's effectiveness.

For customers connected to Lebanon, enhanced EDD procedures apply, including specific verification of the legitimacy of fund flows through the Lebanese banking system, assessment of the customer's connection to Lebanese political or military figures, and specific attention to sanctions compliance given Lebanon's FATF grey-list status.

All employees and relevant third parties receive AML/CFT training appropriate to their role and responsibilities, provided at onboarding and refreshed annually, with additional training provided when there are material changes to the AML/CFT framework, the regulatory environment, or the firm's risk profile.

Token Lebnani implements a regulator-first hiring process for Controlled Function positions designed to ensure candidates meet the highest standards of integrity and competence. All prospective candidates undergo pre-screening against global sanctions lists including the UN Consolidated List, OFAC Specially Designated Nationals List, and EU Sanctions List, as well as enhanced due diligence screening through World-Check or equivalent databases. Finalist candidates are subjected to a rigorous Mock Regulator Interview conducted by Legal Counsel or the Compliance Partner to assess regulatory knowledge and response capability. The platform does not present any candidate for formal FSRA approval without first obtaining an informal no-objection indication from the FSRA Authorisation Division.

Risk Factors and Mitigation

Token Lebnani has identified several material risk factors inherent in its business model and operating environment. Jurisdictional and Enforcement Risk arises from properties being located in Lebanon, requiring cross-border enforcement mechanisms. While arbitration awards are enforceable through the New York Convention and court judgments may be enforced through the Riyadh Arab Agreement or Lebanese reciprocity principles, actual enforcement depends on Lebanese judicial cooperation and may face practical challenges including currency conversion risk at potentially unfavorable exchange rates.

Reliance on FMC Performance is a key operational risk, as the platform's model depends on FMC performance for revenue collection and property maintenance. Specific risks include revenue collection risk in a cash-based environment, commingling risk where property revenues may temporarily mix with FMC operational funds, and operational continuity risk.

Liquidity Risk reflects that secondary market liquidity is not guaranteed. The Matched Redemption mechanism provides liquidity for Retail Clients. Direct peer-to-peer secondary trading is not currently permitted. Investors may be unable to sell tokens at desired prices or times, and Retail Clients should assume they must hold tokens for the life of the investment.

Regulatory Risk acknowledges that the framework for digital securities and cross-border investment continues to evolve. Regulatory changes could affect platform operations, token treatment, or investor access. Specific risks include classification risk, FSRA authorization risk, cross-border regulatory risk, and AML/CFT regulatory risk.

Document 3: Regulatory Analysis — Category 4 (Arranging) Framework

Executive Summary

This regulatory analysis demonstrates that Token Lebnani Ltd's proposed business model falls within the scope of the ADGM Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA) Category 4 Financial Services Permission, specifically the regulated activity of "Arranging Deals in Investments." The analysis establishes that the Category 4 framework is the appropriate licensing pathway for Token Lebnani's primary market issuance activities serving Retail Clients, while clarifying the boundaries that distinguish arranging activities from market operator activities (MTF, Exchange) and custodian activities that would require higher-tier licensing.

Regulatory Framework Overview

ADGM Financial Services Regulatory Authority

The Abu Dhabi Global Market provides the regulatory jurisdiction for Token Lebnani's operations, with English Common Law principles governing commercial relationships and FSRA rules governing regulated activities. This jurisdiction provides significant advantages for cross-border real estate investment, including a well-developed common law framework, sophisticated financial services regulation, and international recognition of ADGM judgments and arbitration awards.

Category 4 License Scope

The Category 4 license authorizes the regulated activity of "Arranging Deals in Investments," which enables Token Lebnani to facilitate primary market issuance of digital securities representing ownership in real estate assets located in Lebanon. Under this framework, Token Lebnani serves as an intermediary that brings together investors and investment opportunities, without taking possession of client assets, operating a trading venue, or providing custody services.

The Category 4 license scope is explicitly limited to arranging activities. Token Lebnani does not operate and is not authorized to operate a Multilateral Trading Facility (MTF), Exchange, or Custodian. This distinction is fundamental to the platform's regulatory positioning and capital structure. The Category 4 framework provides significant capital efficiency advantages compared to higher-tier licenses while enabling the platform to offer institutional-grade investment infrastructure for digital securities.

Arranging Activity Analysis

Definition of Arranging

Under ADGM FSMR Schedule 1, Part 1, Paragraph 25, "Arranging Deals in Investments" encompasses activities where a person brings together parties to a transaction, facilitates transactions between parties, or gives advice in connection with such arrangements. The essence of arranging is the intermediary function—connecting buyers and sellers, issuers and investors—without becoming a principal to the transaction or providing the infrastructure on which trading occurs.

Token Lebnani's Arranging Activities

Token Lebnani's primary market activities fall squarely within the arranging definition:

Facilitation of Primary Market Issuance: Token Lebnani facilitates the issuance of digital securities by bringing together property owners seeking capital and investors seeking property ownership. The platform provides the infrastructure for offering presentation, investor qualification, and transaction execution, but does not purchase securities for its own account or sell securities from its own inventory.

Matched Redemption for Retail Clients: For retail secondary market access, Token Lebnani operates a Matched Redemption mechanism. This mechanism does not involve the platform purchasing securities from its own account. Instead, when a Retail Client seeks to exit, their redemption request is queued and satisfied only when the platform sources matching liquidity from new incoming subscriptions. The existing investor's tokens are transferred directly to the new subscriber, with proceeds from the new subscription funding the redemption. The platform never holds tokens as inventory or acts as a counterparty to Retail Clients.

Investor Qualification and Onboarding: Token Lebnani operates a knowledge-based qualification system verifying that Retail Clients possess requisite understanding of the investment product. This function supports the arranging activity by ensuring that only qualified investors participate in offerings, but does not constitute discretionary portfolio management or investment advice.

Transaction Facilitation: All primary market transactions are executed through the VASP partner infrastructure. Token Lebnani coordinates the transaction process—including offering structure, pricing, and closing—but the actual settlement occurs through the regulated VASP partner, not through Token Lebnani itself.

Contrast with Market Operator Activities

The FSRA distinguishes arranging from market operator activities under Paragraph 29 of Schedule 1, which governs Multilateral Trading Facilities (MTFs) and exchanges. Key distinguishing factors include:

Characteristic Arranging (Category 4) Market Operator (MTF/Exchange)
Order Matching Does not match buy and sell orders Operates order book, matches orders
Trading Venue No continuous trading facility Provides continuous trading
Price Formation Issuer-determined pricing Market-driven pricing
Trading Hours Primary market only Continuous secondary trading
Commissions Arrangement fees only Trading commissions

Token Lebnani explicitly operates within the arranging framework. The platform does not operate an order book, does not match buy and sell orders, does not provide continuous trading functionality, and does not charge trading commissions. The arranging activity is completed once the primary market transaction is executed.

Contrast with Custodian Activities

Custody activities—which would require higher-tier licensing—involve the taking possession or control of client assets for safekeeping, settlement, or management purposes. Token Lebnani operates a strictly non-custodial model, ensuring that the platform never takes possession, control, or custody of investor funds at any stage of the transaction lifecycle.

Non-Custodial Architecture: Investor funds flow from the investor to the VASP Partner's corporate account, through the settlement ledger, to token distribution and owner distribution. Token Lebnani's sole banking relationship is a single corporate account for operational expenses. The platform never touches client funds or assets.

SPV Passivity: The ADGM-incorporated SPVs serve as passive holding vehicles without banking relationships or operational infrastructure. The SPV's sole function is to hold legal title and issue tokens. This passive structure ensures that neither Token Lebnani nor the SPV operates as a custodian of investor assets.

Private Financing Platform Framework

PFP Regulatory Environment

Token Lebnani operates within the Private Financing Platform (PFP) framework applicable within the ADGM RegLab environment. This framework provides specific accommodations for platforms facilitating investments without full prospectus requirements, subject to investor protection conditions.

Retail Client Access

Under the PFP retail exemption, Token Lebnani serves Retail Clients subject to the following parameters:

Maximum of 200 Retail Clients per asset offering

Mandatory knowledge-based qualification (Mastery Assessment)

This exemption enables Token Lebnani to serve the Lebanese diaspora market through appropriate knowledge-based qualification requirements and enhanced suitability and disclosure standards.

Secondary Market Limitations

Category 4 Restrictions

Token Lebnani's Category 4 license explicitly authorizes primary market issuance activities but does not authorize the operation of a trading venue. The arranging activity is completed once the primary market transaction is executed. The Category 4 license does not permit Token Lebnani to operate an order book, match buy and sell orders, provide continuous trading functionality, or charge trading commissions.

Retail Client Secondary Access

Direct peer-to-peer secondary trading for Retail Clients is not permitted under the current license. To provide liquidity options for Retail Clients, Token Lebnani operates a "Matched Redemption" mechanism permissible under ADGM Companies Regulations. Redemption requests are held in a queue and are strictly conditional upon the company sourcing matching liquidity from new incoming subscriptions. Upon receipt of funds from a new subscriber, the company utilizes the proceeds to fund the redemption of the existing investor's tokens.

MTF Partnership Approach

Broader secondary market access is facilitated through partnerships with ADGM-licensed Multilateral Trading Facilities. This approach provides investors with access to established trading infrastructure without requiring Token Lebnani to obtain separate market operator authorization.

Cross-Border Enforcement Framework

ADGM Jurisdiction

The ADGM provides the regulatory jurisdiction for Token Lebnani's operations. Arbitration awards issued in the ADGM are enforceable in Lebanon through the New York Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards (1958), to which both the UAE and Lebanon are signatories. The platform's legal architecture is designed to ensure that all material agreements incorporate ADGM arbitration clauses, enabling dispute resolution within the ADGM framework rather than Lebanese courts, which may face capacity constraints and processing delays.

Court Judgment Enforcement

ADGM court judgments may be enforced in Lebanon under the Riyadh Arab Agreement for Judicial Cooperation (1983) and the principles of reciprocity governed by the Lebanese Code of Civil Procedure. These enforcement mechanisms provide investors with meaningful recourse in the event of property disputes or ownership conflicts, despite the physical assets being located in Lebanon. However, investors should be aware that enforcement of court judgments involves separate legal procedures from arbitration award enforcement and may be subject to additional requirements and timelines under Lebanese law.

Arbitration Currency Provisions

The Arbitrator shall be explicitly empowered to issue awards denominated in US Dollars and to mandate that any payment be made in fresh US Dollars (or equivalent stablecoin) to a designated account outside of Lebanon, to the extent permitted by applicable law. This provision is intended to minimize currency conversion risk during enforcement and to provide investors with recourse that is not subject to Lebanese exchange rate mechanisms.

AML/CFT Regulatory Compliance

Enforcement Litigation Reserve (ELR) Framework

Token Lebnani Limited maintains an Enforcement Litigation Reserve in compliance with GEN Principle 6 and PIB 3 (ICAAP requirements). The ELR is structured as a phased funding model aligned to our risk profile and business growth.

Day 1 Minimum: Upon license issuance, Token Lebnani will maintain a segregated, pre-funded ELR of USD 300,000 in a dedicated operational account. This amount covers a single material enforcement action or litigation event and is consistent with our base case risk assessments for a Category 4 non-custodial platform.

Target Ratio and Growth: The ELR will be increased annually to meet a target ratio of 4% of Total Client Assets Under Arrangement (AUA). This ratio reflects the elevated jurisdictional risk associated with our cross-border virtual asset operations. The full reserve will reach USD 575,000 (our stress-tested threshold) within 36 months of licensing, or sooner if AUA growth triggers the ratio requirement earlier.

Enhanced Due Diligence for Lebanon

For customers connected to Lebanon, enhanced EDD procedures apply, including specific verification of the legitimacy of fund flows through the Lebanese banking system, assessment of the customer's connection to Lebanese political or military figures, and specific attention to sanctions compliance given Lebanon's FATF grey-list status.

Conclusion

Token Lebnani's proposed business model falls squarely within the Category 4 (Arranging) framework. The platform serves as an intermediary facilitating primary market issuance of digital securities representing real estate ownership to Retail Clients, without operating a trading venue, taking custody of client assets, or providing the infrastructure for continuous secondary trading. The Category 4 framework provides an appropriate regulatory pathway that balances the platform's operational objectives with investor protection requirements and capital efficiency considerations.

The platform's non-custodial architecture, passive SPV structure, and clear delineation of arranging activities versus market operator activities confirm that Category 4 licensing is the appropriate framework. Secondary market access for Retail Clients is addressed through the Matched Redemption mechanism and planned MTF partnerships, without requiring Token Lebnani to obtain market operator authorization.

The PFP framework provides appropriate accommodations for Retail Client access, while the platform's risk-based AML/CFT approach and enhanced due diligence procedures address the specific compliance considerations associated with cross-border investments in FATF grey-list jurisdictions. The platform's careful attention to cross-border enforcement mechanisms, including arbitration award enforcement through the New York Convention and court judgment enforcement through the Riyadh Arab Agreement, demonstrates regulatory sophistication appropriate to the complexities of the proposed business model.

Token Lebnani does not hold client money as defined in ADGM COBS Rule 11.2. RESOLUTION AND CESSATION (WIND-DOWN) PLAN

Resolution Framework

The platform maintains a comprehensive resolution framework designed to ensure orderly cessation of operations while protecting investor interests, maintaining regulatory compliance, and preserving the integrity of the SPV structure. This framework addresses potential wind-down scenarios ranging from strategic business cessation to regulatory intervention, ensuring that Token Lebnani can effect an orderly wind-down without prejudice to stakeholder interests regardless of the circumstances precipitating cessation.

The framework recognizes that wind-down scenarios may arise from multiple potential triggers, each requiring different response protocols and timelines. The platform has established procedures that prioritize investor protection, regulatory compliance, and the preservation of value throughout the resolution process. These procedures are designed to provide confidence to regulators, investors, and counterparties that Token Lebnani has thoughtfully considered its obligations in adverse scenarios.

Trigger Events

Wind-down trigger events are categorized into three tiers based on their nature and the urgency of response required. Regulatory triggers include FSRA license revocation or suspension, formal enforcement action that materially impairs operational capacity, and direction from the regulator to cease specific activities or the totality of operations. Financial triggers include capital adequacy falling below regulatory minimums, inability to meet financial obligations as they fall due, and significant losses that threaten the viability of the business model. Strategic triggers include Board determination that continuation is no longer viable, failure to secure necessary regulatory approvals within defined timeframes, and force majeure events preventing continued operations.

Upon occurrence of any trigger event, the SEO shall immediately notify the Board and initiate the response protocol appropriate to the trigger classification. Regulatory triggers receive immediate response with same-day notification to the FSRA and activation of the Standby Servicer. Financial triggers trigger immediate assessment with Board convening within 24 hours to determine response. Strategic triggers follow standard wind-down procedures with Board oversight throughout.

Decision-Making Authority

The Board of Directors maintains ultimate responsibility for wind-down decisions. For trigger events classified as regulatory or financial, the SEO is authorized to initiate immediate protective measures including asset preservation, notification to key counterparties, and activation of the Standby Servicer pending Board convening. The Board shall convene within defined timeframes based on trigger classification to formalize the wind-down decision and oversee implementation.

For strategic triggers, the Board initiates the wind-down through formal resolution, appointing a Wind-Down Manager who assumes operational control of the wind-down process. The Wind-Down Manager may be the SEO, a designated Board member, or an external professional appointed for this purpose, depending on the circumstances of the trigger event and the availability of qualified individuals.

The "Standby Servicer" Model

Upon determination that wind-down is required, a pre-contracted ADGM-licensed Corporate Service Provider (CSP) assumes administrative duties of the Project SPVs. This Standby Servicer arrangement ensures that SPV administration continues uninterrupted regardless of Token Lebnani's operational status, providing continuity of corporate governance and regulatory compliance throughout the wind-down period.

The Standby Servicer relationship is established prior to platform launch through a contractual arrangement that activates automatically upon wind-down trigger. This pre-positioning ensures that no gap exists between Token Lebnani's operational cessation and the assumption of SPV administration by the Standby Servicer. The Standby Servicer is selected based on demonstrated experience with SPV administration, familiarity with the ADGM regulatory framework, and capacity to assume administration on short notice.

Standby Servicer Responsibilities

The Standby Servicer maintains responsibility for four primary functions throughout the wind-down period. Corporate records and filings encompass maintaining SPV statutory registers, filing required returns with ADGM authorities, and preserving corporate documentation in accordance with regulatory requirements. Property management continuity involves coordinating with the incumbent FMC to ensure uninterrupted property operations, monitoring FMC performance during the transition period, and implementing contingency arrangements if FMC continuity is threatened.

Distribution management includes processing investor distributions in accordance with SPV agreements, calculating entitlement to remaining assets, and executing distributions in a manner consistent with the priority established in the wind-down plan. Regulatory liaison encompasses responding to regulatory inquiries, filing required notifications, and coordinating with the FSRA throughout the wind-down period.

The FMC continuity provisions include specific triggers for intervention if FMC performance is threatened during the wind-down period. CLIENT SUITABILITY FRAMEWORK

Retail Client Qualification System

Token Lebnani serves Retail Clients through a comprehensive qualification system designed to ensure that investors possess requisite understanding of the investment product before committing capital. The platform's approach reflects the PFP regulatory framework and ADGM COBS requirements while acknowledging that the target Lebanese diaspora market may not possess traditional financial sophistication but may possess deep familiarity with Lebanese real estate and cultural context.

The qualification system is knowledge-based rather than wealth-exclusive. Rather than requiring all investors to demonstrate High-Net-Worth status (which would exclude the majority of the target diaspora market), Token Lebnani permits participation subject to knowledge-based qualification requirements. This approach expands access to investors who can demonstrate requisite understanding of the investment product while ensuring appropriate protection for those investors who may not possess sophisticated financial backgrounds or professional investment experience.

The qualification system comprises three integrated components. The Mastery Assessment verifies knowledge and understanding of the investment product. The Self-Declaration Protocol ensures informed consent through plain language risk acknowledgment. The 48-Hour Cooling-Off Period ensures considered decision-making by preventing immediate investment commitment.

Mastery Assessment Requirements

The Mastery Assessment is a comprehensive evaluation designed to verify that Retail Clients possess requisite understanding of the investment product. The assessment covers three primary domains that collectively ensure the investor has a complete understanding of the proposition they are entering into.

The first domain is risk recognition, which encompasses understanding of property market risk (including the potential for capital value fluctuation and rental income variability), currency risk (particularly relevant given the Lebanese pound devaluation history and cross-border investment structure), jurisdictional risk (including the challenges of enforcing rights in a foreign jurisdiction), and liquidity risk (the possibility that investors may not be able to exit their investment when desired). Understanding these risks is essential for Retail Clients to make an informed decision about whether this investment is appropriate for their circumstances.

The second domain is asset structure comprehension, which requires the investor to demonstrate understanding of the SPV ownership structure (how the tokens represent beneficial ownership in a special purpose vehicle incorporated in ADGM), token mechanics (how the tokens function as digital securities and what rights they confer), and investor rights (what entitlements the tokens provide and how those rights can be exercised). This structural understanding is essential because the platform's model differs significantly from direct property ownership, and investors must understand exactly what they are purchasing.

The third domain is platform mechanics, which requires understanding of the investment process (how primary market subscriptions work and when tokens will be issued), the distribution mechanism (how rental income and other distributions will be calculated and paid), and secondary market limitations (what liquidity options are available and what restrictions apply). This operational understanding ensures that investors have realistic expectations about the investment experience.

The assessment comprises a minimum of twenty questions drawn from a question bank, with a minimum passing score of 80%. Biometric verification confirms investor identity during assessment completion to prevent proxy testing, ensuring that the person completing the assessment is the same person who will hold the investment. Red-flag questions address fundamental misconceptions that result in automatic failure regardless of overall score, such as misunderstanding the liquidity characteristics of the investment or the jurisdictional nature of the underlying assets.

Retail clients who fail the Mastery Assessment may reattempt after a seven-day cooling period, providing time for reflection and study before a subsequent attempt. Three consecutive failures result in permanent exclusion from the platform for that investor, reflecting the platform's commitment to ensuring that Retail Clients possess the requisite understanding before participating in the investment.

Self-Declaration Protocol

The Self-Declaration Protocol ensures that Retail Clients provide informed consent to the investment through plain language acknowledgment of risks. This protocol addresses the regulatory requirement to obtain necessary information regarding the client's knowledge and experience while acknowledging that the platform is not providing investment advice.

The protocol comprises a Plain Language Summary presented in accessible, non-technical language that summarizes the key risks and characteristics of the investment. The summary is limited to one page and uses clear, straightforward language. Following review of the summary, clients must complete Individual Acknowledgment Questions that require affirmative responses to specific risk acknowledgments. Each acknowledgment is presented as a Yes/No question, and the client must actively confirm understanding before proceeding.

The protocol includes Time Spent Tracking that records the time the client spends reviewing disclosure materials. Clients who spend less than a minimum threshold (e.g., 90 seconds) are prompted to review the materials more carefully before proceeding. This feature provides evidence of genuine opportunity to review and understand the disclosures.

The protocol also includes Comprehension Checks that present brief questions to verify understanding of key points. If a client answers incorrectly, they are directed to review the relevant section before proceeding. This ensures that acknowledgment reflects genuine understanding rather than perfunctory clicking.

All acknowledgment data is retained in an Audit Trail that records what the client acknowledged, how long they spent reviewing materials, and whether they completed any comprehension checks. This evidence supports the platform's position that clients provided informed consent.

48-Hour Cooling-Off Period

The 48-Hour Cooling-Off Period ensures considered decision-making by preventing immediate investment commitment after completing the Mastery Assessment and Self-Declaration Protocol. This period allows Retail Clients to reflect on their investment decision without the pressure of immediate commitment.

The cooling-off mechanism operates as follows. Upon successful completion of the Mastery Assessment (passing score of 80% or above) and the Self-Declaration Protocol (all acknowledgments completed), the client's account is placed in "Pending Investment" status. The client receives immediate confirmation of their qualification status along with a clear explanation that their account will be unlocked for investment in 48 hours.

Within 24 hours of qualification, the client receives a reminder communication summarizing the key risk factors and investment characteristics. This reminder reinforces the information presented during onboarding and provides an opportunity for the client to reconsider if needed.

At the 48-hour mark, the client's account is automatically transitioned to "Active" status, enabling investment. The client receives a "Ready to Invest" notification confirming that their account is active and providing instructions for completing the investment process.

The cooling-off period applies to all Retail Clients regardless of investment amount or previous investment history. The period cannot be waived or shortened under any circumstances, reflecting the platform's commitment to ensuring considered decision-making.

Legacy Investors are diaspora investors seeking to maintain connection to Lebanon through property ownership. These investors typically hold capital outside Lebanon, possess familiarity with Lebanese culture and market conditions, and prioritize stability and capital preservation over aggressive yield maximization. Legacy Investors possess deep familiarity with Lebanese economic history, currency devaluation patterns, and property market dynamics, which informs their understanding of the investment risks and opportunities. This profile represents a core target market for the platform, as the proposition of "hands-free" property ownership without direct management responsibilities aligns well with investors who value connection to Lebanon but may not have the capacity or desire to manage property directly from abroad.

Passive Beneficiaries are investors seeking hands-free property exposure without the management responsibilities associated with direct property ownership. These investors value the platform's operational infrastructure, including FMC management, revenue collection, and distribution services, as an alternative to direct landlord responsibilities. This profile represents a significant segment of the target market, as many diaspora investors have successful careers and personal lives that leave limited time for direct property management but possess sufficient capital to invest in real estate through appropriate channels.

Both profiles reflect the platform's commitment to serving the Lebanese diaspora through accessible, regulated investment products that enable connection to Lebanon without the burdens of direct property ownership.
